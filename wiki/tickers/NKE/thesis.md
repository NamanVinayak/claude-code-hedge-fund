# NKE — Thesis

## TL;DR

Nike remains in a structural downtrend on every relevant timeframe (ADX 39.35, all EMAs bearish, revenue -9.84%, EPS -43.53%). However, the prior directive — **"short on bounces"** (runs `swing_20260411_211655`, `20260417_233350`) — is retired as of run `20260429_220825`. What falsified it: bearish momentum is actively decelerating (daily MACD histogram turned positive, OBV diverging bullishly, daily RSI divergence confirmed), and R/R fails in both directions from current price levels. All five strategy agents independently reached neutral. This is not a reversal thesis; it is a no-trade thesis pending either momentum re-acceleration or confirmed breakout.

---

## What falsified the prior "short on bounces" thesis

The April 11 and April 17 runs identified NKE as the highest-conviction bearish trade in the universe (ADX 64.6, 85% bearish confidence on momentum ranker). That condition is no longer met as of April 29 (run `20260429_220825`):

1. **ADX fell from 64.6 → 39.35** — the extreme trend strength that justified high-conviction shorts has compressed by nearly 40%.
2. **Daily MACD histogram turned positive (+0.52)** — bearish momentum is waning, not accelerating.
3. **Daily OBV diverging bullish from price** — possible accumulation at lows.
4. **R/R fails both directions**: Long entry ~$44.39 to resistance $46.97 = 1.1:1 (below 2:1 minimum). Short entry ~$44.39 to support $42.09 = 0.9:1. The arithmetic eliminated the trade.

## Bear case (still the primary thesis, but no entry)

- **Revenue destruction continues**: Q3 FY2026 revenue -9.8% YoY; EPS -43.5% YoY despite an EPS beat on a low base ($0.35 vs. $0.28 estimate). Nike Direct down 4%, digital -9%, Converse -35%.
- **Macro regime hostile**: 50% tariff threats on Asian-manufactured goods directly attack NKE's cost structure. IMF cut global growth to 3.1%; consumer sentiment under pressure from $4+ gas prices.
- **Competitor acceleration**: Adidas turnaround ahead of schedule (Samba/Gazelle trend), On Running and Hoka in strong momentum. Short interest jumped 22.8% in April to 56.1M shares — institutions agree.
- **Analyst consensus deteriorating**: Split 17 Buy / 17 Hold / 2 Sell (down from prior Buy majority). Analyst consensus does not expect annual sales growth return until February 2027.
- **Valuation not cheap**: DCF fair value ~$15B vs. ~$65.7B market cap (77% premium); owner-earnings analysis 96% overvaluation (source: `20260429_220825` valuation_analyst_agent).

## Bull case / what could flip this to a long

- **Insider conviction**: Board directors (Swan $500K, Rogers $173K, Cook $2.9M) bought at multi-year lows near $42–43. This creates a "squeeze ignition" risk above those levels.
- **EPS beats on low base**: Four consecutive EPS beats, most recent Q3 FY2026. If this pattern drives a sentiment reversal before fundamentals recover, short sellers get squeezed.
- **P/S at 1.42, P/E at 29.2x** — historically low for Nike; the market may anticipate a 2027 turnaround before it appears in numbers.
- **Elliot Hill's "Win Now" strategy**: Any sign of Nike Direct inflection or major product sell-through would break the bear thesis.

## What would restore an actionable setup

**Short re-entry**: MACD histogram resumes expansion negatively + ROC 5d/10d both turn negative + price fails at the $45–47 resistance zone on volume.

**Long entry (reversal thesis)**: Close above $46.97 on 1.5×+ volume + EMA alignment beginning to flatten/turn bullish. This has not occurred as of April 29.

**Q4 FY2026 earnings (June 25)** remains the next binary catalyst. Until then, NKE is a monitor, not a trade.
