# NKE — Thesis

## TL;DR

Nike is in a confirmed structural downtrend — revenue shrinking, EPS collapsing, competitors accelerating, and management in flux. Two consecutive swing runs (Apr 11 and Apr 17, run IDs `swing_20260411_211655` and `20260417_233350`) reached the same conclusion: short on bounces. The lone counterargument is insider buying at multi-year lows, which opens a tail-scenario for a violent mean-reversion bounce that could damage a short.

---

## Bear case

The bearish thesis is broad and multi-layered (sources: `swing_20260411_211655`):

- **Revenue destruction**: Q3 FY2026 revenues flat YoY; Nike Direct down 4% reported, 7% currency-neutral to $4.5B. Revenue growth: -9.8% YoY. EPS growth: -43.5% YoY.
- **Competitor gap widening**: On Running posted +24.9% YoY sales; Hoka +11.1%. Adidas is ahead of schedule on its 4-year turnaround. Nike is losing shelf space in running and streetwear simultaneously.
- **Management instability**: Chief Innovation Officer Tom Bignall departed after less than a year (April 10, 2026). Combined with prior leadership churn, this undermines confidence in the turnaround story under CEO Elliot Hill.
- **Analyst consensus deteriorating**: Wave of April downgrades — Piper Sandler cut to Neutral ($50 target), DA Davidson cut to Neutral ($46 target), Citigroup cut target to $53. Analysts don't expect annual sales growth to return until February 2027.
- **Elevated tariff exposure**: Footwear manufacturing in Asia faces tariff headwinds; elevated inventory compounds margin pressure.
- **Valuation still not cheap on fundamentals**: Valuation agent (`swing_20260411_211655`) reached 100% bearish confidence — DCF fair value ~$15B vs. ~$63B market cap (76% premium); owner-earnings analysis shows 96% overvaluation. Even at decade lows, the stock was not cheap on fundamental measures as of April 2026.
- **Trend confirmation**: ADX at 64.6 is among the strongest trend readings in any ticker analyzed. Momentum ranker gave NKE the highest-conviction bearish signal (85% confidence) of any ticker in the Apr 11 universe.

## Bull case / what could flip this

The bull case is thin but real enough to respect in position sizing:

- **Insider conviction at the lows**: Board director Robert Swan bought $500K on Apr 7, John Rogers Jr. bought $173K on Apr 9, Tim Cook bought $2.9M in December. Inside-director accumulation at multi-year lows is a rare contrarian signal.
- **Extreme oversold readings**: Z-score of -2.06 (well beyond -2 threshold), RSI ~22, stochastic ~2. Mean-reversion agent (`swing_20260411_211655`) rated this 68% bullish bounce probability based solely on statistical extremity.
- **52-week low valuation optionality**: Stock back to levels from over a decade ago. Some analysts calling it a "generational buying opportunity" (Motley Fool, Apr 6 2026). P/S at 1.36 and P/E ~19-28x (depending on forward EPS) is historically low for Nike.
- **'Win Now' turnaround**: CEO Elliot Hill's refocusing strategy may produce results in FY2027-2028. If any segment shows inflection, the short thesis breaks.

## What would change the bear thesis

1. Two consecutive quarters of Nike Direct revenue growth (>5% reported, not currency-neutral)
2. ADX dropping below 30 and EMAs beginning to flatten or cross bullish
3. Insider buying accelerating to >$5M in a single quarter
4. Any major competitor stumble (e.g., On Running's rapid expansion faces margin pressure)
5. Successful turnaround catalyst — major product launch with genuine sell-through data, not just hype
