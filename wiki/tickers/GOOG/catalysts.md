## Recent news synthesis

**Antitrust relief (positive).** On April 8, 2026 the court issued its remedy: a choice-screen mandate and prohibition on exclusive search distribution contracts — not forced divestiture. Alphabet stock rallied on the news (20260415_093758 web_research, headline: "Alphabet dodges structural breakup"). However the DOJ immediately filed a cross-appeal seeking stronger remedies, so the tail risk of divestiture remains alive.

**Search index sharing order (negative).** April 14, 2026: US District Court ordered Google to share its search index with rivals. Morgan Stanley estimates $15–25B in annual ad revenue at risk if rivals build competing search products on top of the index (20260415_093758 web_research).

**Waymo expansion catalyst (positive).** Nashville expansion sent GOOG up 7.3% in a single session leading into the swing_20260411 run. Waymo is unmodeled by consensus — each new city is incremental optionality.

**Google Cloud AI partnerships (positive).** Thoma Bravo / Google Cloud AI partnership flagged in both runs as a fresh enterprise catalyst. Google partnered with Intel for AI cloud infrastructure (swing_20260411_211655 web_research). Google Cloud Next conference ran April 22–24 in Las Vegas — expected to generate further pipeline announcements.

**Capex overhang (negative).** $175–185B 2026 capex guidance, roughly double year-on-year, weighed on the stock. GOOG fell nearly 9% in the week ending March 27, 2026 after this guidance was revealed (20260415_093758 web_research). Bears argue this compresses free cash flow and may not yield returns for 2–3 years.

**AI competition (negative, ongoing).** Microsoft MAI-Transcribe-1 outperformed Gemini audio on the FLEURS benchmark (April 2, 2026). Meta's Muse Spark and OpenAI's enterprise push are both direct Gemini competitors (20260415_093758 web_research, competitor_activity field).
