# AAPL — Catalysts

## TL;DR

The single biggest near-term catalyst is Q2 FY2026 earnings on April 30, 2026. The market is pricing in uncertainty on two axes: (1) can Services and iPhone hold up margins under a tariff regime, and (2) will Apple's AI strategy close the gap to Samsung/Google. Both research runs (20260411_211655 and 20260415_093758) flagged earnings as a binary event that could resolve the chart in either direction.

## Recent news synthesis

**Tariff mitigation — constructive.** Apple airlifted 1.5M+ iPhones from India and is committed to shifting most U.S.-bound iPhone production there by end of 2026. Management guided Q2 revenue growth of 13–16% YoY ($107.8B–$110.7B). (Source: web_research/AAPL.json, run 20260415_093758.)

**Tariff risk — still real.** Under a full 145% tariff scenario, Ming-Chi Kuo warns iPhone gross margin falls from ~47% to 2–3%. Current tariffs are temporarily at 30% for 90 days — any escalation back toward 145% invalidates Q2 guidance. (Source: web_research/AAPL.json, run 20260415_093758.)

**AI strategy mixed signals.** Positive: billion-dollar annual Google deal to power Siri via Gemini; iOS 27 will allow rival AI assistants. Negative: Siri 2.0 delays may push to iOS 27 in September — a full cycle behind Samsung Galaxy S26. (Source: web_research/AAPL.json, runs 20260411_211655 and 20260415_093758.)

**Services expansion.** Apple TV now available as paid add-on on Amazon Prime Video (US). MacBook Neo (A18 Pro) targeting the $32B entry-level laptop market. (Source: web_research/AAPL.json, run 20260411_211655.)
