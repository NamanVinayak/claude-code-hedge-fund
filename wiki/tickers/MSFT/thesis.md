# MSFT — Thesis

## TL;DR

Microsoft is one of the highest-quality businesses in public markets — durable recurring revenue, dominant market positions in cloud, productivity, and developer tools — currently trading roughly 30% below its all-time high due to concerns about AI infrastructure spending and sector rotation out of tech. Three swing runs (Apr 11, Apr 15, Apr 17) all saw the model flip bullish once technicals confirmed a bottom; the Apr 17 run placed a buy at $420.26.

## Bull case

- **Azure is re-accelerating.** Q2 FY2026 showed Microsoft Cloud crossing $50B in revenue for the first time (+26% YoY). Analyst consensus for Q3 FY2026 (earnings April 29) expects Azure growth of 37-38% constant currency. Eight consecutive EPS beats reinforce execution credibility. - **Valuation reset is historically cheap.** As of Apr 11, MSFT traded at 23.7x P/E — the cheapest in years. The pullback trader noted a textbook Fibonacci 78.6% retracement as a buy level. Bernstein maintained an Outperform rating with a $641 price target; average analyst price target across 31 analysts was $582 with zero Sell ratings. [source: web_research/MSFT.json, runs swing_20260411_211655 and 20260415_110848]
- **Copilot monetization is expanding.** GitHub Copilot reached 20 million developers. Azure AI Foundry processed 500 trillion tokens YoY (7x growth). New Copilot capabilities for legal, finance, and compliance were announced April 15, 2026 — meaningful enterprise market expansion. [source: web_research/MSFT.json, run swing_20260411_211655]
- **Custom silicon reduces cost dependency.** The Maia 200 AI accelerator chip is positioned as 40% cheaper than Amazon Trainium3 and Google TPU Ironwood, reducing long-term infrastructure cost and cloud margin pressure. [source: web_research/MSFT.json, run 20260415_110848]
- **Infrastructure investment is strategic, not speculative.** The 3,200-acre data center campus purchase in Cheyenne, Wyoming signals long-horizon conviction. Capital deployed now creates barriers to entry that compound over years.

## Bear case

- **AI capex is massive and front-loaded.** Microsoft's ~$145B annualized infrastructure spend rate (vs AWS $200B, Alphabet $175-185B) is significant. If AI monetization doesn't accelerate fast enough, margins compress. The valuation agent flagged MSFT 100% bearish on this concern in the Apr 15 run. [source: explanation.json, run 20260415_110848]
- **OpenAI partnership tension.** An April 13 CNBC memo revealed OpenAI said the Microsoft partnership "limited our ability to meet enterprises where they are," with OpenAI now touting an Amazon/Bedrock alliance. Loss of exclusive OpenAI distribution would remove a key AI moat. [source: web_research/MSFT.json, run 20260415_110848]
- **Sector rotation headwind.** As of April 11, energy (+21% YTD), industrials (+12%), and staples (+15%) were all outperforming tech. Money was rotating into value, with enterprise software specifically flagged as an outflow sector. Goldman Sachs published a negative report on software stocks. Stifel downgraded MSFT to Hold in February. [source: web_research/MSFT.json, run swing_20260411_211655]
- **Copilot execution risk.** The Apr 11 run noted that Copilot AI assistant was reportedly in "Code Red" emergency overhaul mode — suggesting product-market fit is not yet proven at scale despite impressive developer adoption numbers.

## What would change my mind

**Bull to bear:** Azure growth decelerates below 30% in Q3 earnings, Copilot monetization metrics disappoint, or OpenAI formally terminates the Microsoft partnership in favor of AWS.

**Bear to bull (already happened):** Daily SuperTrend flipping bullish (confirmed in Apr 15 run with `trend_changed=true`), multi-agent consensus shifting to 6/9 bullish (Apr 15 run), and price recovering toward the $400-$420 zone.

**Ongoing watch:** Each earnings print is the clearest signal. MSFT has beaten EPS 8 straight quarters; a miss would likely re-test the $370 support zone.
