## Recent news synthesis

**Positive:**
- **Meta $60B infrastructure deal** — Multi-year commitment covering AMD GPUs, Venice-architecture CPUs, and custom silicon co-engineering. Described by analysts as transformational for AMD's Data Center revenue visibility (source: web_research/AMD.json, run 20260415_110848, Capital.com/Motley Fool, April 2026).
- **TSMC 2nm first adopter** — CEO Lisa Su confirmed April 15, 2026 that AMD will be first on TSMC's 2nm node. Ahead of both Nvidia and Intel on next process generation (source: web_research/AMD.json, run 20260415_110848).
- **Q4 2025 beat** — Revenue $10.27B (+34% YoY), Data Center $5.38B record, EPS $1.53 vs $1.24 estimate. Shares fell on soft Q1 sequential guidance despite the beat (source: web_research/AMD.json, run 20260415_110848).
- **AMD server CPU share crosses 40%** — Pricing power at Intel's expense, validated by multiple analyst reports (source: web_research/AMD.json, run swing_20260411_211655).
- **Goldman Sachs top pick** heading into Q1 earnings season; Erste Group upgraded to Buy (source: web_research/AMD.json, run swing_20260411_211655).

**Negative:**
- **25% tariff on advanced AI chips** — Enacted January 14, 2026, covers AMD MI325X and Nvidia H200. Raising GPU/memory prices, compressing margins on hyperscaler deals (source: web_research/AMD.json, run 20260415_110848, Axios/CNN Business).
- **Nvidia market share gap widened** — Nvidia holds ~89% server GPU share despite MI-series ramp. AMD's addressable market is smaller than the headline AI tailwind implies (source: web_research/AMD.json, run 20260415_110848, IDC/April 2026).
