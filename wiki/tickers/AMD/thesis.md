# AMD — Thesis

## TL;DR

AMD is a confirmed AI infrastructure winner. The bull thesis — Meta $60B deal, TSMC 2nm first-mover, hyperscaler CPU market share gains, and structural data center GPU demand — has been validated by the April 2026 parabolic run from ~$200 to $353 ATH (+76% in 21 days). The thesis is not in question; timing and entry are the entire debate. ## Bull case

**AI infrastructure tailwinds are durable.** Data Center revenue hit $5.38B in Q4 2025 (+39% YoY), and Q1 2026 consensus projects ~$9.8B total revenue (+32% YoY). AMD CEO Lisa Su stated April 27 that AMD is "only in year 2 of a 10-year AI build out" (source: web_research/AMD.json, 20260430_140858). Hyperscaler demand is structural, not cyclical.

**The Meta deal is transformational.** Meta committed a $60B infrastructure deal covering AMD GPUs, Venice-architecture CPUs, and custom silicon co-engineering (source: thesis.md bootstrap, run 20260415_110848). Multi-year revenue lock-in at a time when AMD is gaining CPU server share at Intel's expense.

**TSMC 2nm first-mover advantage.** AMD confirmed as first company on TSMC's 2nm process ahead of both Nvidia and Intel (source: web_research/AMD.json, run 20260415_110848). Manufacturing moat that could widen the MI-series gap.

**Lowered bar for Q1 earnings.** AMD pre-guided Q1 softly after Q4 2025 beat ($1.53 EPS vs $1.24 estimate), setting consensus EPS at $1.24–$1.27. Intel's Q1 +22% Data Center & AI revenue is a positive read-through for AMD (source: web_research/AMD.json, 20260430_140858). Beat probability elevated.

**Analyst conviction broadening.** DA Davidson upgraded to Buy, raised PT to $375 (April 29). 36 Buy / 24 Hold / 0 Sell among analysts. Avg PT $296.24 but bull range $375–$380 (source: web_research/AMD.json, 20260430_140858).

## Bear case

**Extreme valuation.** P/E 132.31, P/S 18.03, P/B 9.74 (source: fundamentals_analyst_agent, run 20260430_140858). Valuation agent 100% bearish. DCF intrinsic value ~$32.8B vs market cap $560.9B — a 94% gap (source: valuation_analyst_agent, run 20260430_140858). Near zero margin for error at each earnings print.

**Near-term R/R unfavorable.** At current price ~$337–$345, upside to immediate resistance ($352.99) is ~2.3%; downside to key support ($328.81) is ~4.7% — a 0.5:1 ratio. Even extending to Fib 1.272 at $366.67 yields 1.3:1. Below the 2:1 minimum threshold (source: swing_macro_context, run 20260430_140858).

**Sell-the-news risk on earnings.** Stock is near Evercore ISI's $358 bull target. A beat-and-lower Q2 guidance scenario (tariff headwinds, Nvidia 89% server GPU share) could trigger a sell-the-news collapse despite a headline beat (source: swing_catalyst_news, run 20260430_140858).

**Tariffs.** 25% tariff on advanced AI chips including MI325X. Ongoing margin headwind not fully passed through on multi-year hyperscaler contracts.

**Insider selling.** CEO Lisa Su sold ~$16M in March 2026. Board distribution 2:1 sellers vs buyers (source: swing_catalyst_news, run 20260430_140858).

## What would change my mind

**Bull to neutral/bear:** Q1 earnings miss on Data Center revenue (below $5B), any hyperscaler contract cancellation, MI450 delay beyond Q3 2026, or tariff expansion to all AMD chip categories.

**Bear to bull on timing:** Confirmed daily close above $352.99 with 1.5x+ daily volume (breakout trigger), OR pullback to $308–$315 with hourly MACD re-crossing bullish and ADX maintaining >40 — the setup becomes asymmetric at that zone (~17.5:1 R/R at $312 entry per head trader, run 20260430_140858).
