# META — Thesis

## TL;DR

Meta is the world's largest social advertising platform, crossing $200B in annual revenue for the first time in FY2025 (source: web_research/META.json, run 20260415_093758). The core bull case is that AI-powered ad tools and Reels/WhatsApp monetization expand margins faster than the $115–135B capex plan compresses them. The bear case is that the capex is value-destructive and a wave of social-media addiction litigation creates open-ended liability. Our model bought META twice across the April 2026 run cycle — one paper trade filled, one paper trade was abandoned before fill.

## Bull case

**Structural ad dominance.** eMarketer projected META will surpass Google in global digital ad revenue for the first time in 2026, capturing 26.8% of worldwide ad spend ($243B vs. Google's $239B). Advantage+ AI ad tools are driving measurable ROI improvements (source: web_research/META.json, run 20260415_093758).

**Reels and WhatsApp monetization still early.** Reels ad rates are catching up to feed rates; WhatsApp business messaging is "early-stage with massive upside." Both are multi-year tailwinds the current multiple underprices.

**Proprietary AI transition.** The April 8, 2026 Muse Spark launch — led by Alexandr Wang after a $14.3B investment for a 49% stake in Scale AI — marks Meta's pivot to competitive proprietary AI. Muse Spark scored 4th on AI Index v4.0 and integrates into Facebook, Instagram, WhatsApp, Messenger, and Ray-Ban glasses. Consumer-scale distribution is the moat no pure AI lab can replicate.

**Analyst consensus unusually strong.** 61 of 67 analysts rated Buy/Strong Buy as of mid-April 2026; consensus target ~$860, implying ~47% upside from ~$585 (source: web_research/META.json, run 20260415_093758). Q4 2025 EPS of $8.88 beat estimates by 8.16%; FY2025 revenue crossed $200B for the first time.

## Bear case

**Capex overhang.** The $115–135B 2026 capex plan is nearly double FY2025's $72B. If AI monetization is slow, free cash flow compresses sharply. Investors have already responded: a $300B market cap was erased between late 2025 and March 2026, driving the stock ~28% below its $796.25 all-time high.

**Legal liability is open-ended.** A California jury found Meta 70% liable in a social media addiction trial (March 2026), with 10,000+ similar lawsuits and 100,000+ individual arbitration claims pending nationwide. New Mexico awarded $375M in a child exploitation case. Massachusetts AG lawsuit was cleared to proceed April 10, 2026. The 2026 10-K warns this could significantly impact financials (source: web_research/META.json, run 20260415_093758).

**EPS trend was declining at time of entry.** The swing explanation from run 20260415_093758 noted EPS trend was -4.5% and the insider sell/buy ratio was the worst in the basket (668 sells vs. 240 buys). This is fundamentally a momentum-and-catalyst trade, not a deep value trade.

**Multiple compression risk.** With the S&P forward P/E at historically elevated levels and the Fed on hold until December 2026, any AI capex ROI disappointment could trigger another leg down.

## What would change my mind

**Bull to bear:** Large social media addiction settlement (>$10B), or two consecutive quarters of capex above $140B with flat ad revenue growth. Hard landing macro compresses ad budgets.

**Bear to bull:** Q1 2026 earnings beat with raised guidance and any capex discipline signal. A litigation settlement framework removing open-ended liability would be a structural re-rating catalyst.
