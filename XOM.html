Thesis (from Wiki)

ExxonMobil is a high-quality integrated major with genuine structural growth (Guyana Stabroek, lithium diversification, Pioneer synergies) but its near-term stock price is hostage to the oil macro. The ceasefire-driven unwinding of a $30+/barrel geopolitical premium is the dominant short-term force, and three consecutive model runs (Apr 11, Apr 15, Apr 17) all reached the same conclusion: short on bounces until the macro picture stabilizes. Analyst consensus is nominally bullish (avg target $154–$159) but that reflects pre-ceasefire price targets that assume elevated crude prices hold.

Guyana barrels are high-margin and structurally growing independent of Middle East dynamics.
